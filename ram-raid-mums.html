Ram Raid Mums - A Hard-Hitting Documentary

A compelling documentary directed by Brandon Te Moananui for TVNZ, produced by Awa Films Ltd. This work delves into the complex social issues surrounding youth involvement in ram raids, offering a nuanced and humanizing perspective on the mothers and families affected. It showcases Brandon's ability to tackle complex, hard-hitting subjects with a unique and insightful perspective, characteristic of his urban Māori lens.

Director's Vision

Brandon's directorial approach for 'Ram Raid Mums' focuses on bringing an authentic urban Māori lens to a sensitive and often misunderstood subject. The vision is to move beyond sensational headlines and explore the human stories behind the statistics, fostering empathy and understanding. By highlighting the experiences of the mothers and families, the documentary aims to shed light on the systemic issues and intergenerational challenges that contribute to youth involvement in such activities, encouraging a broader conversation about community, support, and pathways to positive change.
